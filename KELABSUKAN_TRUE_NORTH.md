# KelabSukan True North

## Purpose

This document is the single source of truth for KelabSukan product direction. Read this before making product, UI, technical, commercial, or roadmap decisions.

KelabSukan is the grassroots sports media, story, stats, and recognition platform for amateur sports communities.

Short-term positioning:

> Strava + sports newsroom for Malaysian racket communities.

Long-term positioning:

> The platform where amateur athletes become visible.

KelabSukan should not be built as ordinary club management software. It should make ordinary sports sessions feel important: stories, drama, rivalries, rankings, player profiles, photos, videos, match reports, weekly recaps, and talent visibility.

## Core Belief

Clubs, admins, and members stay free.

They are not the first customers to charge. They are the community, the content, the culture, and the attention layer.

Revenue should come from the attention around them:

- advertisers
- sponsors
- merchants
- venues
- brands
- event partners
- sports service providers

KelabSukan monetizes like grassroots sports media, not SaaS.

## Mission

KelabSukan turns amateur sports activity into:

- stories
- drama
- rivalries
- rankings
- stats
- player profiles
- club history
- media moments
- future hype
- talent visibility

The product should make amateur players feel seen, remembered, recognized, and worth following.

## Product Loop

Every session should create a media cycle:

1. Before session: generate hype.
2. Session happens.
3. Scores, attendance, and media are added.
4. Stories and match reports are generated.
5. Session recap is published.
6. Stats, rankings, and profiles update.
7. Next-session tension is created.
8. Stories are shared back into WhatsApp and public pages.

Core loop:

> before session -> hype -> session happens -> scores/media uploaded -> stories generated -> recap published -> stats updated -> next-session tension created

## Product Pillars

### Members

Members get free profiles, stats, rankings, match history, stories, media moments, and recognition.

The player should feel like they are building a sports identity, not doing admin work.

### Clubs

Clubs get free pages, sessions, scores, club history, member activity, and media coverage.

The club should feel like its own league with its own newsroom.

### Stories

Stories are the product. Stats are the proof.

KelabSukan should automatically create:

- match reports
- session reviews
- rivalry updates
- player spotlights
- weekly recaps
- champion stories
- comeback stories
- talent-watch stories

### Audience

Public opt-in content should build audience:

- public club pages
- player profiles
- match reports
- public rankings
- rising players
- tournament coverage
- latest sports feed

People should visit kelabsukan.com to see what is happening in amateur sports.

### Commercial

Sponsors and advertisers pay for attention around the community.

The community stays free.

## Experience Direction

KelabSukan should feel like:

- Strava for amateur sports identity
- ESPN or The Athletic for local amateur stories
- a club newsroom
- a player career file
- a public grassroots sports feed

It should not feel primarily like:

- admin software
- a generic booking system
- a spreadsheet replacement
- a boring SaaS dashboard

## Pro Sports Media Lessons

KelabSukan should learn from how NBA, NFL, MLB, NHL, and other major sports turn games into media businesses.

The lesson is not to copy their budget. The lesson is to copy their media logic.

Major sports create value by turning every game into:

- pre-game hype
- player storylines
- rivalry tension
- stats debates
- match reports
- highlight moments
- player rankings
- future predictions
- sponsor inventory
- fan conversation

KelabSukan should bring this treatment to amateur sports.

### Player As Media Asset

Professional sports market individual players, not just teams.

KelabSukan should do the same:

- every player has a career card
- every player has form, rivalries, trophies, and history
- every player can become a story
- every player can be followed, shared, featured, and remembered

The player card is the starting point for recognition, sponsorship, scouting, and public attention.

### Session As Media Cycle

Professional sports do not only cover the result. They cover before, during, after, and what comes next.

KelabSukan should structure every session as:

- preview
- players to watch
- rivalry watch
- match reports
- session recap
- weekly awards
- next-session hook

The platform should make one session create content for the next session.

### Stats As Story Fuel

Stats should not be presented as dry numbers.

Stats should answer:

- who is rising?
- who is under pressure?
- who owns the rivalry?
- who is improving?
- who is the best partner?
- who is the player to watch?
- what record might fall next?

Stats are proof. Story framing makes them valuable.

### Personal Stakes

Fantasy sports and professional media make fans care because there are stakes.

KelabSukan should create personal stakes without gambling:

- ranking position
- win streak
- rival record
- partnership form
- weekly champion
- player card reputation
- talent-watch visibility

Users should open the app because something personal might have changed.

### Fans And Players As Distribution

Professional leagues use creators, fans, highlights, and social platforms for distribution.

KelabSukan's first distribution network is WhatsApp.

Every important story should be easy to send back into WhatsApp as:

- story text
- image card
- public proof link
- player card
- session recap
- rivalry update

## First Market

Start with racket sports, especially Malaysian badminton communities.

Initial supported sports:

- badminton
- tennis
- pickleball
- squash
- table tennis
- racquetball

Long-term, the same story engine can expand to other amateur sports.

Do not overbuild multi-sport too early, but do not hardcode badminton-only concepts everywhere.

## WhatsApp Principle

WhatsApp remains the discussion layer.

KelabSukan is the structured proof, story, stats, and memory layer.

The app should always support:

- WhatsApp invite links
- WhatsApp-ready story text
- shareable scorecards
- shareable rivalry cards
- shareable session recaps
- public proof links

Do not try to replace WhatsApp chat.

## Story Engine

V1 story detection can use existing data:

- matches
- score sets
- participants
- events
- RSVPs
- leaderboards
- Elo
- comments/reactions
- uploaded media later

Story types:

- close match
- clean sweep
- comeback win
- win streak
- losing streak broken
- champion dethroned
- first win over rival
- rivalry tied
- nemesis continues
- unbeaten pair
- best partner
- weekly champion
- most active player
- rising player
- player to watch

Stories must always link back to proof.

## Session Media Engine

Every completed session should generate a review when enough data exists.

Session review should include:

- headline
- top story
- match reports
- player of the session
- rivalry update
- dynamic duo
- biggest surprise
- leaderboard movement
- uploaded photos/videos
- next-session hook

The platform should create continuity from past sessions to future sessions.

Examples:

- "Can Amir respond after losing the rivalry?"
- "Will the unbeaten pair survive another week?"
- "Husni is one win away from a 5-match streak."
- "The rematch everyone asked for."

## Player Career Cards

Every player should have a portable career card, similar in spirit to a baseball card.

This card should follow the player everywhere:

- dashboard
- player profile
- match reports
- rivalry pages
- story cards
- public sports feed
- tournament pages
- sponsor/media showcases
- WhatsApp shares

The player card is not just decoration. It is the player's sports identity.

Core card details:

- player photo/avatar
- display name
- primary sport
- club/team
- city/location
- current rank or rating
- win/loss record
- current form
- signature stat
- best partner
- top rival/nemesis
- trophies or badges
- latest headline moment
- career highlight

The card should evolve over time as the player records more sessions, matches, media, and stories.

Possible card modes:

- **Basic Card:** name, club, sport, record, rank.
- **Story Card:** latest headline, moment proof, share CTA.
- **Rivalry Card:** head-to-head record against another player.
- **Tournament Card:** event result, seed/rank, form.
- **Talent Card:** rising player, improvement trend, standout stats.
- **Sponsor-Safe Card:** clean public version for featured stories and commercial placements.

Design principle:

> The player card is the atomic media asset of KelabSukan.

If a player becomes known, followed, sponsored, scouted, or remembered, it starts from this card.

Soft talent showcase language:

- Player to Watch
- Rising Name
- Breakout Week
- Strong Form
- One to Follow
- Most Improved
- Fastest Climber

Avoid making hard scouting claims too early.

## Weekly Build Cadence

Each release should make the app feel more alive, more personal, or more shareable.

Near-term weekly sequence:

1. **Backbone Cleanup:** fix lint/test failures, refresh status docs, stabilize score entry and sharing basics.
2. **Player Career Card V1:** reusable player card with avatar, club/sport, record, rank/Elo, form, best partner, nemesis, and latest headline.
3. **My Sports Story Dashboard:** latest personal story, career card, next-session hype, recent proof, and share CTA.
4. **Moment Cards:** reusable cards for close match, streak, rivalry, dynamic duo, weekly champion, and rising player.
5. **Session Review:** automatic session headline, top moments, match reports, player of session, and next-session hook.
6. **Club Sports Desk:** club newsroom with latest story, match reports, weekly awards, rivalry watch, and media highlights.
7. **Public Showcase:** opt-in public stories, featured clubs, rising players, and rivalry of the week on the landing page.
8. **Media Upload:** manual photo/video uploads attached to sessions, story cards, and recaps.
9. **WhatsApp Share System:** share text and image export for every meaningful story/card.
10. **Sponsor Slots Pilot:** small manually configured sponsor placements on story cards and weekly recaps.

Rule:

> Do not ship a week of invisible admin-only work unless it directly unlocks stories, audience, trust, or shareability.

## Technical Direction

Use computed story objects first. Persist stories only when needed for history, notifications, public feeds, or performance.

Suggested computed shape:

```ts
type StoryMoment = {
  id: string
  type: string
  scope: 'player' | 'club' | 'rivalry' | 'pair' | 'session'
  title: string
  summary: string
  proof: string
  clubId: string
  eventId?: string
  matchId?: string
  playerIds: string[]
  mediaIds?: string[]
  shareText: string
  createdAt: string
}
```

Keep sport-specific rules isolated in a story/rules layer.

Use generic terms where possible:

- match
- session
- team
- player
- opponent
- partner
- streak
- champion
- moment
- story

Use sport-specific labels through configuration when needed.

## AI Strategy

AI should act like a club journalist, not a generic chatbot.

AI can later help with:

- headline suggestions
- match report writing
- weekly recap writing
- rivalry summaries
- player spotlight drafts
- interview questions
- tone rewriting
- "on this day" memories
- sponsor-safe copy variants

AI must not:

- invent facts
- create fake scandals
- publish unverified accusations
- expose private data
- auto-generate harmful personal attacks

Deterministic rules produce the facts. AI polishes the storytelling.

## Guardrails

- Public showcase is opt-in.
- Private club data stays private.
- Payment status is never public.
- Contact details are never public.
- Sponsored content must be clearly labeled.
- Stories must be based on verified sports activity.
- No private-life gossip.
- No personal insults.
- No body or appearance jokes.
- No sensitive political, religious, or health speculation.
- No unverified scandals.
- Youth/player privacy needs removal and visibility controls.

Drama is allowed when it is sport-based, playful, and backed by data.

## Brutal Risks

- If players do not record scores, stories cannot exist.
- If stories feel cringe, people will not share.
- If privacy is mishandled, trust dies.
- If public pages have no traffic, advertisers will not pay.
- If the app feels like admin work, WhatsApp wins.
- If sponsors cannot measure value, revenue will be weak.
- If every match becomes noisy news, the product loses taste.

## Decision Filter

Before building any feature, ask:

- Does this build member habit?
- Does this create or improve stories?
- Does this grow audience?
- Does this protect trust and privacy?
- Does this support free clubs, admins, and members?
- Does this help future sponsor/media revenue?
- Does this make amateur athletes more visible?

If the answer is mostly no, the feature is probably not core.
